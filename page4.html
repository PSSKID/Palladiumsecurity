Retail Solutions
Usually found in big box stores

When you are looking to automate your home, education of the different technologies available is very important to ensure a reliable and stable automation system that will work under all conditions.

Most devices sold in the retail space are Wi-Fi based technologies. These devices range from plug in wall receptacles, wall switches, light bulbs, doorbells and locks. In most part, these devices work well once installed but the downfall of these devices is the Wi-Fi portion of the technology and lack of integration with security systems.

Most home networks are not created to have this type of technology working on a 24/7 clock.

We won’t get too technical about networking, but we will try to explain what the downfall of using strictly Wi-Fi based products for home automation means.

The function of a router is to manage the traffic between all your networked devices as well as ROUTING traffic of your internal devices to the world wide web. In short, your router is the data traffic controller of your network.

This appliance and how it handles all this heavy data may result in poor results for everything reliant on your internet service.

When you create a network in your home, you must take a few things into consideration.   

• How many devices will I have running on it? These can include computers, tablets, cellphones, printers, streaming devices, video game systems, televisions and home assistants.  

• How many of these devices are wireless?  

• Can the wireless router handle video streaming for services like Netflix and gaming at the same time?

• How many people will be using these devices at the same time?

• Is my internet subscription adequate for the data I will be using?

When purchasing a Wi-Fi router, the selection decision can come down to price, brand or what the salesperson offers. If you select the wrong product, all your Wi-Fi products can lag causing poor performance.
